INCIDENT REPORT

Date of Incident: March 3, 2026
Time: 10:45 AM
Location: Zone A, Foundation area
Project: Riverside Office Complex

TYPE: Recordable Injury (First Aid + Medical Treatment)

DESCRIPTION:
Worker (J. Hernandez, Rebar crew) sustained a laceration to left forearm
when rebar tie wire snapped during placement. Worker was wearing required
PPE including gloves, but the wire end penetrated through the glove material.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. First aid administered on site
2. Worker transported to Riverside Medical Center
3. 6 stitches received, worker returned to site on modified duty
4. Area inspected and secured

ROOT CAUSE: Defective tie wire batch (higher carbon content than specified)
CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
- Wire supplier changed manufacturers without notice
- Pre-use inspection of wire not performed per SOP

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
1. [OPEN] Replace all wire from batch #TW-2026-0445 - Due: March 7
2. [OPEN] Reinstate pre-use material inspection per SOP 4.2 - Due: March 6
3. [OPEN] Issue toolbox talk on wire handling hazards - Due: March 7
4. [COMPLETE] Notify wire supplier and request documentation

OSHA RECORDABLE: Yes (Medical treatment beyond first aid)
